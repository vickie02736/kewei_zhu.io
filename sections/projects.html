Selected Projects

These projects represent the current core directions of my research program and are organized by research theme.

Theme 1: Chemical Representations

ReadMOF

Problem: Materials ML often depends on structural graphs or handcrafted descriptors, while systematic MOF names contain rich chemical signals that are underused.

Approach: ReadMOF learns language-based representations from chemical nomenclature and combines them with structure information for property prediction.

Impact: The model improves interpretable and scalable screening of framework materials for targeted applications.

Theme 2: Atomistic Machine Learning

TrumbleMOF

Problem: Conventional interatomic potentials struggle with long-range interactions and flexibility in porous framework materials.

Approach: TrumbleMOF uses transformer architectures to model complex atomic environments and nonlocal interactions in MOFs.

Impact: It enables more accurate and scalable atomistic simulation for materials discovery and analysis.

BuildMOF

Problem: De novo discovery of high-performing MOFs remains expensive due to the combinatorial search space.

Approach: BuildMOF explores multimodal and targeted generative modelling to propose candidate structures that satisfy desired constraints.

Impact: The project supports faster design cycles for functional framework materials.

Theme 3: Surrogate Modelling for Simulation

Reduced Modelling for Unstructured Mesh

Problem: High-fidelity simulations on unstructured meshes are computationally expensive and limit exploration speed.

Approach: This work develops reduced-order surrogates that learn from numerical simulation data while preserving key physical behaviour.

Impact: It accelerates scientific computing workflows for complex systems.

P-STMAE

Problem: Spatiotemporal physical systems are difficult to model when data is sparse and dynamics are multiscale.

Approach: P-STMAE combines masked autoencoding and physics-aware constraints to learn robust temporal representations.

Impact: The framework improves data efficiency and predictive stability for scientific time-series modelling.
