Research Themes and Projects

A theme-first view of my research journey, connecting published work with the directions that continue to shape how I think about materials, modelling, and discovery.

Theme 1: Learning Chemical Representations of Materials

This theme studies how chemical language, symbolic descriptions, and structural context can be fused into machine-readable representations for materials discovery.

Research Continuity

ReadMOF

Established

Research Focus: New chemical representations that let AI read MOFs through both language and structure.

How I Work: ReadMOF builds language-centered models and aligns them with structure-aware materials modelling.

Why It Matters: It opens a more interpretable and scalable route from chemical description to property prediction.

BuildiMOF

In Preparation

Research Focus: Generative workflows for proposing promising MOF candidates under targeted design goals.

How I Work: Multimodal and conditional generation guided by materials behavior and scientific constraints.

Why It Matters: It extends representation learning from understanding materials to imagining new ones.

Mapped Publications

  • Augmented Reality for Enhanced Visualization of MOF Adsorbents - JCIM
  • Analyzing drop coalescence in microfluidic devices with a deep learning generative model - PCCP

Theme 2: Machine Learning for Atomistic Interactions

This theme develops scalable models for long-range and complex atomistic interactions, linking modern deep learning architectures to physically meaningful simulation tasks.

Research Continuity

TrumbleMOF

In Progress

Research Focus: Rich atomistic learning for porous, flexible, and long-range interacting materials systems.

How I Work: Transformer-based atomistic modelling with geometry-aware context for MOFs.

Why It Matters: It supports more realistic and scalable simulation in materials workflows.

BuildiMOF (Atomistic Conditioning)

In Preparation

Research Focus: Keeping generative materials design chemically meaningful and atomistically grounded.

How I Work: Coupling generative proposals with atomistic constraints and simulation-aware objectives.

Why It Matters: It bridges creative AI generation with physically grounded validation.

Mapped Publications

  • Accurate identification and measurement of the precipitate area by two-stage deep neural networks in novel chromium-based alloys - PCCP
  • Machine learning for modelling unstructured grid data in computational physics: a review - Information Fusion

Theme 3: Surrogate Models for Physical Simulation

This theme is the methodological backbone of my publication trajectory: from multiphase and microfluidics modelling to reduced simulation and general scientific surrogate learning.

Research Continuity

Reduced Modelling for Unstructured Mesh

In Progress

Research Focus: Faster scientific simulation on irregular geometries and unstructured meshes.

How I Work: Reduced-order and data-driven surrogate models that preserve the physical character of the system.

Why It Matters: It makes simulation more accessible for exploration, iteration, and downstream scientific use.

P-STMAE

Established

Research Focus: Data-efficient modelling of spatiotemporal scientific systems under physical constraints.

How I Work: Physics-aware masked autoencoding for robust representation learning of dynamics.

Why It Matters: It pushes surrogate modelling toward broader scientific foundation models.
