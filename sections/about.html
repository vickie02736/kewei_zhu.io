Research Statement

I build machine learning methods for modelling materials across chemical, atomistic, and continuum scales, with a focus on framework materials such as MOFs.

My current work connects three directions: chemical representation learning, atomistic interaction modelling, and surrogate models for large-scale simulation. The goal is to make materials modelling faster, more accurate, and more scalable for real scientific discovery.

Biography

I am a PhD student at University College London (UCL), supervised by Prof. Peyman Z. Moghadam and Prof. Mojtaba Abdi-Jalebi.

My current work integrates scientific machine learning, representation learning, and physics-informed modelling for AI4Science applications.
