Research Statement

I am excited by the idea that AI can help us read, simulate, and design materials in ways that are both expressive and physically meaningful.

  • Chemical and multimodal representations: helping AI understand MOFs and related materials through language, structure, and scientific context.
  • Physics-aware modelling: building atomistic and surrogate models that stay close to the behavior of real physical systems.
  • Generative discovery workflows: turning scientific insight into design tools that can guide faster and more meaningful materials exploration.
  • Collaboration with experimental groups: working with experimental researchers to connect AI models with validation, synthesis, and practical materials discovery.

Biography

I am a PhD student at University College London (UCL), supervised by Prof. Peyman Z. Moghadam and Prof. Mojtaba Abdi-Jalebi.

My work sits at the intersection of scientific machine learning, materials modelling, and AI4Science. What excites me most is building methods that remain grounded in physical intuition while supporting more reliable and meaningful materials discovery.
