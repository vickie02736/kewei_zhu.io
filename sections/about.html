Research Statement

I develop machine learning methods for understanding and simulating complex physical systems across chemical, atomistic, and continuum scales, with a focus on framework materials such as MOFs.

A central challenge in computational materials science is that physical phenomena span multiple representations and scales—from chemical composition and bonding, to atomistic interactions, and ultimately to continuum-level physical behaviour. Traditional modelling approaches treat these scales separately, which limits our ability to efficiently predict materials properties and accelerate materials discovery.

My work explores how machine learning can bridge these scales by learning expressive representations of materials structures and physical interactions. I develop models that allow AI systems to read chemical structures, learn atomistic forces, and build surrogate models for large-scale physical simulations.

Through this approach, my research aims to enable faster and more scalable modelling of functional materials, supporting applications such as electronic materials discovery, porous materials design, and large-scale physical simulations.
