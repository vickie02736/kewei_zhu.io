About me

I work on machine learning methods for understanding and predicting structure–property relationships (SPRs) in metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), with a focus on physically grounded and data-efficient representations.

I am broadly interested in:

  • Representation learning for MOFs including graph- and geometry-aware models as well as chemical language–based representations (e.g. systematic chemical descriptions), and their interaction for capturing chemical and physical constraints.
  • Data-driven structure-property modelling such as adsorption behaviour, electronic properties, and stability, with an emphasis on benchmarking, robustness, and generalisation across materials classes.
  • Scientific machine learning for materials where latent-space modelling and physically informed objectives enable learning from limited, noisy, or irregular data.

I am a PhD student at University College London (UCL) since 2023, under the supervision of Prof. Peyman Z. Moghadam and Prof. Mojtaba Abdi-Jalebi.
