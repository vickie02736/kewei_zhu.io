Research Themes

My research program is organized around three connected themes that bridge chemical representations, atomistic modelling, and large-scale simulation.

1. Learning Chemical Representations of Materials

Materials understanding starts from expressive and interpretable representations. I study how chemical language and structure-derived descriptors can capture framework chemistry for property prediction.

Associated project: ReadMOF

2. Machine Learning for Atomistic Interactions

Many key properties emerge from atomic-scale interactions that are expensive to simulate directly. I develop scalable ML interatomic potentials, including transformer-based models for long-range effects in porous materials.

Associated project: TrumbleMOF

3. Surrogate Models for Physical Simulation

Large scientific simulations on complex geometries are often computationally intensive. I build reduced and surrogate models that preserve essential physics while accelerating downstream analysis.

Associated project: Reduced Modelling for Unstructured Mesh
